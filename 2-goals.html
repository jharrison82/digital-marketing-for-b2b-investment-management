Ultimately, a common goal of all investment managers is to grow their assets under management (AUM) over time. Generating sales (inflows) of the firm’s products and preventing redemptions (outflows) of money already invested – alongside any rise in the prices of existing investments – contributes to the firm’s AUM growth.

In the business-to-business (B2B) world – in contrast to business-to-consumer (B2C), where marketing can go directly to the end investor – the "heavy lifting" is the responsibility of the sales team. Visitors to a B2B investment management firm's website don't open investment accounts or invest in funds online. Instead, the firm’s sales teams maintain long-term relationships with investment professionals at other businesses and are responsible for generating sales of the firm's products.

Any trades in or out of the firm's products are either handled directly by the sales team or conducted entirely through a third-party investment platform (for example, a financial advisor may use a platform that offers a range of funds, selected from various investment managers, to invest their clients' money). As transaction and holdings data isn't consistently available, it is very difficult – I would say almost impossible – to attribute marketing activity to specific buy and sell orders of the firm's products (despite this, I've seen it attempted a lot, with little success).

Therefore, we will focus our digital marketing efforts on a more general level of support for the sales team, unconcerned with any attempt to credit the marketing team's involvement in specific inflows of money.

At the core of the strategy is the firm's marketing automation database, which we will attempt to grow over time with a mix of new leads and existing clients. The digital activity of individuals within the marketing automation database can be tracked at an individual level (such as an identified contact's website visits, email engagement, downloads, and form submissions). This information can then be fed to salespeople to help them create new relationships and support their ongoing prospecting and client servicing efforts.

Underpinning this is the involvement of the digital marketing team in the production and distribution of marketing content, both to salespeople – for use with their clients and prospects – and directly to the firm's audiences. In addition to arming the sales team with valuable resources, it encourages digital activity of clients and prosects that can be fed back to the sales team.
