Languages

In this chapter
  • Introduction

In this chapter, we will define which languages the firm communicates in digitally.

While an investment manager with a multilingual audience would like to communicate with every individual in their preferred language (which can sometimes be a regulatory requirement), it's a huge task: communicating in multiple languages can add many extra levels of complexity and the required translation work is very labour intensive and expensive — although, increasingly, technology can help.

Therefore, a balance must be struck with available resources. It's important to be realistic, so the team doesn't bite off more than it can chew, and we need to identify up front which languages the firm plans on communicating in. This makes it clear what maintenance work will be required and allows us to determine how any added complexity will be addressed.

Several areas should be covered:

  • Core website. The static pages of the firm's website (such as the home page).
  • Regularly published website content. Any website pages or content that will be added on a regular basis (such as blog posts, articles, press releases, reports, videos, and webcasts).
  • Marketing materials. Any materials produced by the marketing team.
  • Regulatory documents. Any materials which must be made available in certain languages for regulatiry reasons (for example, certain documents relating to UCITS funds must be made available in the local languages of any country where they are registered for sale).
  • Distribution channels. Social media, email, advertising.

As technology is becoming increasingly advanced, there are several areas where it can help: website browsers can translate pages, YouTube can use speech recognition technology to automatically create captions for videos, and so on. However, using these won't make your audience feel served, and in other areas — such as marketing materials — technology is less able to help. As I said, it's a balance based on the reosurces of the firm. In some cases, it can be less complicated to decide that certain documents will be translated into multiple langugaes as standard practice rather than making a decision for each.
