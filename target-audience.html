Target Audience

In this chapter, we will define the target audience for your firm. We will look at the typical clients of B2B investment managers, how your firm structures its sales teams, and the specific types of investment professionals your salespeople want to talk to.

Target organisations

The typical organisations that B2B managers work with are displayed in figure 1. They are split between two client groups (or channels): institutional and intermediary.

Institutional client group

The institutional client group includes institutions and the institutional investment consultants & OCIOs who provide them with investment advice.

Institutions

The are organizations that need to invest money on behalf of others — or for the institution itself. Examples include pension funds (who need to invest money coming from contributions of plan participants), insurance firms (from premiums paid by customers), or sovereign wealth funds (from oil revenues). Often, they have some form of internal investment committee or board of trustees that decides overall investment strategy and hires investment managers to manage portions of the institution's portfolio.

A more comprehensive list of institutions includes public and corporate pension funds, family offices, endowments, foundations, central banks, sovereign wealth funds, and insurance firms. There are others — such as hedge funds, mutual funds, and REITs — but these don't seem to be typical clients of B2B investment managers.

Institutional investment consultants & OCIOs

Institutions that lack investment expertise can outsource the work to an institutional investment consultant or a provider of outsourced chief investment officer (OCIO) services.

Both consultants and OCIOs can help with asset allocation (dividing the institution's portfolio between asset classes) and manager selection (choosing which investment managers to hire for each allocation). However, unlike consultants, OCIOs can typically make discretionary decisions on the institution's behalf — and so an institution can essentially outsource its entire investment team by hiring one.

Confusingly, some of the larger investment managers offer OCIO services alongside the more specialised providers.

Intermediary client group

The intermediary client group includes the financial advisor firms who provide advice to retail investors and the investment platforms who serve both advisors and retail investors.

Financial advisors

These organisations provide various forms of financial advice to retail investors, as either an independent firm or part of a larger organisation — such as the wealth management department (or subsidiary) of a bank.

Although the specific services they provide and the level of wealth of their clients can vary, any firms that provide financial advice to retail investors — such as wealth managers, private banks, and financial planners — are included here.

Investment platforms

A platform allows groups of retail investors or financial advisors to buy and sell a range of investment products — offered by a variety of investment managers — in one place.

Financial advisors use platforms to manage their clients' portfolios, and retail investors use them to manage personal investments (where they are sometimes known as fund supermarkets).

Sales team structure

To understand how your firm allocates sales coverage across client groups and target organisations, we can look at how the sales teams are structured internally.

Although it depends on the firm's size, B2B investment managers usually have multiple sales teams — or individual salespeople — that are assigned to cover a client group, target organisation, or geographic area (or various combinations). Geographic areas include individual countries (France, Spain, or Germany), a group of countries (the Nordics, DACH, or Benelux), or parts of a country (a state or region within the United States).

Some investment managers also have a dedicated team for their most important clients identified at an account (or firm) level, known as strategic accounts.

To help fill any gaps in sales coverage, client service or sales support teams sometimes cover relationships with clients or prospects who are located in any geographic area which isn't covered by existing sales teams or salespeople.

Lewisham's sales team structure

The sales team structure of Lewisham Asset Management (our fictional investment management firm) is detailed in figure 2.

At the top level, the sales team is split across two main geographic areas: United States and International. Within the United States, teams are then split by organisation type, with each salesperson in the intermediary team also assigned a specific region. Internationally, a single team covers both intermediary and institutional relationships, with individual salespeople covering specific countries or regions; another team covers consultant relationships outside the United States. One salesperson within the international team has been assigned to cover intermediary and institutional relationships with individuals in regions that don’t have sales coverage – such as Asia or the Middle East.
