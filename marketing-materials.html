Marketing Materials

In this chapter
  • Introduction

In this chapter we will perform an audit of existing marketing materials, review how they are hosted, ensure that downloads can be tracked in the marketing automation system, and make all documents easily accessible by the sales teams.

Document audit

Marketing teams at investment managers usually create and maintain a range of standard materials about the firm and the products and services it offers. In addition to being used for marketing activity, salespeople share them with their clients and prospects.

We will identify which documents are currently being produced.

Investment strategy and vehicle materials

The first step is to identify the types of documents the marketing team creates for each of the firm's individual investment strategies and vehicles, which may include:

  • Factsheets. Produced periodically (monthly or quarterly) with information on portfolio holdings and investment performance.
  • Pitchbooks/presentations. A detailed document covering the investment philosophy & process, people, performance, and the investment case. Some firms have software that allow salespeople to create their own customised documents, usually by choosing slides from a library, but we are only concerned with standardised materials.
  • Flyers. A short document (usually around 1 or 2 pages) using key content from the pitchbook/presentation.
  • Commentaries. Produced periodically (monthly or quarterly) with performance commentary, attribution, any changes to portfolio holdings, and an outlook.

Most of these document types will be produced on a one-to-one basis: a single document for each investment strategy or vehicle. For pooled funds, however, some documents — such as the factsheet — are typically published separately for each share class.

Once we have identified the exisiting document types, we can check which specific documents are being produced for each individual investment strategy and vehicle, using the lists created in the products & services chapter as a handy reference. Sometimes, especially in smaller teams, resource contraints limit how many can be maintained.

Other materials

Next, we should identify any other standard materials, which should be more straightforward. These could include firm level documents (such as a corporate brochure, presentation, or firm factsheet), any materials that cover multiple investment strategies or vehicles (such as a brochure for a fund range or model portfolios), or any other materials you consider relevant.

Regulatory documents

Finally, you may wish to include some key regulatory documents in the audit, such as a prospectus, annual report, or semi-annual report (required documents for each range of pooled funds) or a key investor information document (KIID) or key investor document (KID) (required documents for UCITS share classes). Although they can be quite dry, they are often used by prospects seeking information before they make investment decisions.

However, this can be a large task, so consider it carefully.
